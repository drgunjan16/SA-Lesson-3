Introduction to Gametes and Fertilization

  • Female gamete (egg, n)
  • Male gamete (sperm, n)
  • Fertilization/Zygote (2n): Formation of diploid offspring containing homologous pairs of chromosomes.

Anatomical and Physiological Parts of Sperm

  • The whiplike movements of the tail propel the sperm.
  • The midpiece contains mitochondria that provide metabolic energy for motility.
  • The head has the father's chromosomes (genetic contribution).
  • The acrosome is a sac covering the sperm’s head, containing enzymes that assist in fertilization.

Anatomical and Physiological Parts of the Ovum

  • Surrounded by two layers: zona pellucida (glycoprotein matrix, barrier to sperm) and corona radiata (external follicular cells nourishing the egg).
  • The cytoplasm (ooplasm) contains centrosome, centriole, mitochondria, and cortical granules.

Fertilization Sequence

  1. Preparation of the sperm: Capacitation in the female tract.
  2. Sperm-zona binding: Species-specific receptor-ligand interaction.
  3. Acrosome reaction: Release of digestive enzymes (acrosin, hyaluronidase) for drilling through the zona pellucida.
  4. Sperm-egg fusion: Involves fertilin, formation of male pronucleus, and final diploid nucleus.
  5. Egg activation: Cortical reaction and zona hardening prevent polyspermy.

Molecular Events in Fertilization

Effect of Capacitation

Sperm membrane becomes capable of acrosome reaction and binding to the zona pellucida after exposure to the female reproductive tract.

Acrosome Reaction

  • Outer acrosomal membrane fuses with sperm plasma membrane, releasing enzymes.
  • Penetration of the zona pellucida enables sperm fusion with the oocyte.
  • Sperm incapable of this reaction cannot fertilize intact oocytes.

Sperm-Egg Fusion

  • Tip of sperm head contacts egg plasma membrane, initiating fusion.
  • Sperm drawn into egg, nucleus forms male pronucleus, fuses with female pronucleus to restore diploid chromosome number.

Egg Activation & Cortical Reaction

  • Prevents multiple sperm entry.
  • Cortical granule exocytosis hardens the zona pellucida via Ca2+ signals, blocking additional sperm.

Summary of Sperm-Egg Fusion Process

  1. Hundreds of sperm approach and break through corona radiata.
  2. Contact with zona pellucida triggers acrosome reaction—enzymes digest zona matrix.
  3. A single sperm fuses with the oocyte plasma membrane and delivers its nucleus.

Site of Human Fertilization

  • Fertilization usually occurs in the fallopian tube (ampulla region).
  • After ovulation, the egg travels through infundibulum to the ampulla where it meets sperm.

Assisted Reproductive Technologies (ART)

  • In Vitro Fertilization (IVF): Eggs stimulated and retrieved, fertilized with sperm in vitro, then introduced into uterus.
  • Zygote Intrafallopian Transfer (ZIFT): Eggs fertilized in vitro, then zygotes transferred into fallopian tube.
  • Gamete Intrafallopian Transfer (GIFT): Eggs and sperm placed together in a fallopian tube for natural fertilization.
  • Intracytoplasmic Sperm Injection (ICSI): A single sperm injected directly into the egg during IVF.
  • Intrauterine Insemination (IUI): Sperm injected into uterus artificially (no sexual intercourse).

Twins

  • Identical (monozygotic): Division of a single zygote into two embryos.
  • Fraternal (dizygotic): Two eggs fertilized by two different sperm.

Learning Objectives

  • Identify male and female reproductive organs and their functions.
  • Describe key events and hormones in the female reproductive cycle.
  • Identify parts of the sperm and ovum relevant for fertilization.
  • Describe normal fertilization including capacitation, acrosome reaction, binding, and fusion.
  • Describe post-implantation development (morula, blastocyst, implantation, placentation, gastrulation).
  • List three germinal layers and their fates.
  • Describe fetal development in the three trimesters and common developmental defects.
  • Explain ART methods, the process of implantation, placenta role, and abnormalities.
  • Assess infertility, implantation issues, trimester effects of toxins, and ART consequences like multiple births.
  • Explain procedures like PGD/PGS in ART and discuss ethical concerns.
